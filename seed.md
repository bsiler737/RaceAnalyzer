We are making an analysis tool that helps bike racers understand the results of road races in the Pacific Northwest. 
Results are available on the site https://www.road-results.com/
The Pacific Northwest is defined as the states of WA, ID, OR and the province BC
We want to look at results data to find characteristics of each race. For example, some races will have
* Sprint finishes, where the group generally finishes together but the winner has a strong sprint
* Breakaway finishes, where a small group finishes in front of the main pack
* Individual finishes, where a hill splits the peloton apart completely and people finish individually or in small groups (rare in the Pacific northwest area

The goal of this analysis is to help racers know what to expect and how to plan their strategy for the best outcome for them and their team

For each race, racers want to know
* What is the most common type of finish? 
* What is the most common type of finish for my selected category (such as P12, masters men, masters men 40+, women 123, etc.)
* What is the frequency of that kind of finish (most of the time, some of the time)
* Given the race predictor results from road-results.com, what kind of a finish do you expect for upcoming races based on past results? How likely is that outcome
* If I think I am a sprinter/roleur/attacker/time trialist/other cycling phenotype, which races are good for me in the PNW?
* How many times has a given finish type happened at this race? What is the "trend" of finish types (like maybe a UI element showing the finish type for the last 5 years)
* Given the expected finish type and the people currently registered in the race predictor from road-results, who should we watch for as the top 10 or so candidates to finish in the podium? Provide probablities for a top 3 podium finish and a win
